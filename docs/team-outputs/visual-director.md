## 3. Styling Rules (15 Rules)

**ADD before every shoot:**
1. Wool throw (charcoal or oatmeal) folded on sofa arm or foot of bed - single most impactful prop
2. Two ceramic mugs - one on coffee table, one on terrace - Hario V60 or any handmade CZ pottery (Tvorilka, Keramika Boleslaw)
3. 3 books: one Gestalten travel atlas (Mountains, Alps/Dolomites), one architecture/design book (spine visible), one fiction in Czech or English - stacked on coffee table or sofa
4. Single eucalyptus or olive branch stem in bathroom - in a small ceramic or simple glass
5. Small pine cone or two on coffee table or window ledge - zero cost, totally on-brand

**REMOVE before every shoot:**
6. TV remote - hide in drawer. Every photo.
7. All plastic bottles visible anywhere (shower gel, kitchen, cleaning supplies)
8. Red Alessi key fob on entrance wall shelf
9. Any charging cables or power strips
10. Owner personal items (toothbrush, medication, toiletries)

**MOVE / REARRANGE:**
11. Open all terrace and window curtains fully before every interior shot - the mountain view is a selling point, not a privacy risk in photos
12. Move a chair or stool to the terrace before terrace shots - empty outdoor space reads as "forgotten"
13. Arrange sofa cushions in a loose triangle, not lined up hotel-style
14. Make bed with linen tucked tight at foot, pillows plumped, with one corner turned down and throw across the foot
15. Turn on all interior lights including both paper lanterns - warm interior glow is essential even in daytime shots

**cabin_trae-style props that work in CZ context:**
- Patagonia or Arc'teryx cap hung casually on entrance hook (not the current red plastic fob)
- Fjallraven Kanken or Osprey pack leaning against a wall (active lifestyle signal)
- Hario V60 or Aeropress on kitchen counter
- Czech atlas or mountain hiking map folded open on coffee table
- Wool Merino throw from Woolroom or Czech brand Vlnika
- Pine cone cluster in a small ceramic bowl
- One jar of local honey or mountain herbal tea from Beskydy/Jizera range

## 4. Camera + Lighting Brief

### Camera
**Smartphone is borderline sufficient for the detail/atmosphere shots only.** For hero shots, bedroom, living room, and exterior: hire a mirrorless. The current phone shots already show the limit - the bedroom ceiling shots have noise in the shadows and the living room has barrel distortion from the phone ultrawide. For a listing at this price point (likely 100-150 EUR/night based on the market), bad photos are costing 30-40% in conversion.

Recommended minimum: Sony A7 series or Fuji X-T series with 16mm f/2.8 (rooms) and 35mm f/1.8 (details). A friend with a Fuji or Sony mirrorless shooting in 2-3 hours will beat a phone reshoot every time.

### Lens Strategy
- **16-18mm equivalent** - rooms, terrace, exterior (keep level, no tilting up)
- **35mm equivalent** - sofa/bed 3/4 angle, feels natural and non-distorted
- **50-85mm equivalent** - detail shots only (mug, book stack, pine knot texture)

### Lighting: Natural Only
No flash. No ring lights. No LED panels.

| Shot Type | Time | Light |
|---|---|---|
| Bed hero | 08:00-09:30 | East-facing soft morning |
| Living room wide | 15:00-17:00 | Warm afternoon through terrace doors |
| Terrace lifestyle | 07:30-09:00 or 17:30-19:00 | Low sun angle |
| Exterior with warm windows | 20min after sunset | Blue hour + interior lights on |
| Detail shots | Any overcast | Diffuse, no hard shadows |
| Bathroom | Mid-morning when window light is in | Soft ambient |

**Rule:** If shadows are harsh (midday sun), reschedule. Overcast is your friend for interiors.

### Post-Processing
- Warm white balance (+200-400K from phone default - the pine wood should look honey-golden, not yellow)
- Lift shadows by 20-30% - the apartment films darker than it looks in person
- Reduce vibrance by 10%, not saturation - keeps the sage green correct without going neon
- True black for the TV credenza and entrance joinery - do not crush it to grey
- No HDR, no sky replacement, no AI upscaling artifacts
- Target look: warm + slightly underexposed highlights, lifted shadows - the TRÆ edit is never blown out

## 5. Restage Budget Options

### Option A: Owners DIY (0-1,200 CZK + one Saturday morning)
**What to buy:**
- Wool throw: Ikea FRILUFT or H&M Home (500-700 CZK)
- 2x ceramic mugs: Tvorilka.cz or Hofer/Lidl ceramics (200 CZK)
- 3 books: second-hand or already owned (0-300 CZK)
- 1 eucalyptus bunch: flower market or Ikea (50 CZK)
- Pine cones: pick up outside (0 CZK)

**Phone reshoot approach:** Use iPhone Portrait mode for detail shots. Use wide mode for rooms but stand back more - shoot from a low-hip height (not face height). Shoot only in the morning or overcast. Total time: 2-3 hours.

**Result: 5.5/10 vs TRÆ.** Meaningful step up from current 3.5, but not converting at premium rates.

### Option B: Mirrorless friend + styling (1,500-3,000 CZK)
Find a friend with Sony/Fuji/Nikon mirrorless. Offer them a free weekend stay or 2,000 CZK gift. Budget 200 CZK for props (pine cones, eucalyptus, one mug). Plan a Saturday morning shoot (3 hours). Owner does the styling prep the night before using this brief.

**Result: 7/10 vs TRÆ.** Good enough for AirBnB conversion lift and credible Instagram.

### Option C: Czech professional interior photographer (12,000-25,000 CZK)
Czech interior photography market has good options at this price point. A few names worth approaching:

- **Jakub Skokan / Studio Skokan** (Praha) - shoots for Czech boutique hotels and Airstream-style listings, understands Scandinavian aesthetic
- **Ondrej Kocourek** (Brno) - architectural photography, has done Czech cabin/mountain properties
- **Search:** Czech Union of Architectural Photographers (CZAP) roster; filter for "interiery ubytovani" in their portfolio tags

Day rate covers 20-30 final processed images. For this property size (1-bedroom, terrace, exterior), half-day rate (~12,000-15,000 CZK) is sufficient. Ask to include one blue-hour exterior session.

**Result: 9/10.** Investment pays back in ~5-8 bookings at a premium nightly rate.

**Recommendation: Start with Option B. Run it for 60 days, track conversion. If CTR improves, justify Option C for next season.**

## 7. What NOT to Photograph

- TV as the subject of any shot (angle away or exclude entirely)
- TV remote, power strips, charging cables, WiFi router
- Supermarket plastic bottles (water bottles, cleaning products, shower gel)
- Anything with a visible consumer brand logo (except deliberate lifestyle props)
- The entrance from outside the front door (looks like any apartment building)
- Mirror shots that include the photographer
- Bed with smoothed-flat hotel-duvet styling (too corporate - use linen wrinkle)
- Overhead drone shots of the parking lot or access road
- Any photos taken at 12pm sun (flat, harsh, cold)
- Children's items unless explicitly targeting families
- Generic "tidy kitchen with all appliances" - kitchens should be styled minimally (one mug, one cutting board, that is all)
- Owner storage (ski boots in the corner, jacket collection, shoe racks full)

## 8. Pre-Shoot Checklist

Run this top-to-bottom on the morning of every shoot before picking up the camera:

1. **Windows and glass clean** - fingerprints and condensation are invisible in person, fatal in photos. Wipe all terrace doors, bathroom mirror, shower glass.
2. **Bed made properly** - fresh linen, tucked at foot, pillows plumped + stacked, throw folded across foot of bed. Takes 10 minutes and is the single highest-ROI prep action.
3. **All cords hidden** - TV, phone chargers, lamps, kitchen appliances. Every one.
4. **No plastic visible** - bathroom shelves, kitchen counter, entrance. Remove to a cupboard.
5. **All curtains and blinds fully open** - every room, before first shot.
6. **Terrace swept and clean** - no leaves, no bird mess, chairs positioned intentionally (not pushed against wall).
7. **All interior lights on** - both paper lanterns, any under-cabinet kitchen lights, bathroom light. Even in daylight - they add warmth.
8. **Props placed** - throw on sofa, mugs on coffee table + terrace, books stacked, eucalyptus in bathroom, pine cones on coffee table.
9. **Daylight check** - is it overcast or golden hour? If harsh noon sun: reschedule or pull sheer curtains to diffuse (not close - diffuse).
10. **Walk through as a guest** - stand in the entrance and look in. Does it look like a place you would pay to stay? If anything bothers you in person, it will bother the camera more. Fix it now.
